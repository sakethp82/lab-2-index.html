Evolving Cybersecurity of AI-Featured Digital Products and Services

By Andrea Renda, Florent Frederix, and Nikolaos Isaris

Abstract

The field of cybersecurity has changed dramatically since the Cybersecurity Strategy for the Digital Decade was presented by the European Commission and the High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy in December 2020. The Cybersecurity Strategy highlights the potential of AI as a new technology, but also the need for cyber security of AI technology. Indeed, since the strategy was adopted, AI has shown that it has enormous potential for growth, but also several risks and vulnerabilities that this new technology brings. The paper analyses the shift and further development in the field of cybersecurity of digital products and services, AI itself as a technology, as well as products and services that will contain an AI component.

Introduction: Digital Resilience from Organisations to Products and Services

To build trust in digital services and products, including elements such as AI technologies, they must be properly designed and provide an acceptable level of security and privacy. Cybersecurity plays a key role for all stakeholders in building trust in digital products and services. Surveys show universal agreement on the importance of digital trust.

Conclusion

Technical standards have become increasingly significant in the legal framework of the European Union, particularly in the context of cybersecurity and AI regulation. Although formally voluntary, harmonized standards effectively serve as binding elements, shaping the interpretation and application of legal requirements.

Addendum: Future Challenges in Cybersecurity for AI

The rapid evolution of AI technologies will continue to present new challenges in cybersecurity. Future efforts must focus on integrating ethical guidelines, ensuring data privacy in AI applications, and providing transparent security certifications for emerging AI systems. Moreover, the collaboration between governments, industry leaders, and research institutions will be pivotal in establishing effective global standards for AI cybersecurity.
